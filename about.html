A brief History

Found by Fusajiro Yamuchi in Kyoto 1889, Nintendo was orignally a producer of hand made Hanafuda playing cards. After almost a century as more hobbies became available, card sales drop and Nintendo expanded its business to table top games like Chess, Shogi, Go and Mahjong. Finally in 1973, Nintendo released its first console "The Color TV game". Ever since then Nintendo has been producing more consoles and games.

Our Consoles

Game & Watch (1980)

Released in 1980, the Game & Watch was inspired by the thought of a watch that could play games. Overall the game had one game installed and supported 2 versions of the game. The Game & Watch originally released with Ball, but later different versions of the console released with different games.

Nintendo Entertainment System (1983)

Originally releasing in Japan, as Famicom and later released in 1985 in America. The Nintendo Entertainment System was designed to be a cheap home entertainment system. The console featured interchangable game cartridges and followed a controller design similar to the Game & Watch.

GameBoy (1989)

The GameBoy is our newest handheld console that contains features from both the Game & Watch and the NES. This handheld features a screen with adjustable contrast, 5 control buttons and interchangable game cartridges`
